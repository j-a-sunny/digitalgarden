A process control system (PCS) is a set of technologies, including sensors, controllers, and actuators, that monitor and adjust industrial variables—such as temperature, pressure, or flow rates—in real-time to maintain optimal, safe, and consistent production. These systems automate manufacturing operations, ensuring they adhere to predefined parameters and reducing human error.

**Key Components of a Process Control System**

- **Sensors/Transmitters:** Measure process variables (e.g., thermocouples for temperature, pressure sensors).
- **Controllers:** Analyze data from sensors, compare it against the desired set point, and calculate necessary adjustments.
- **Final Control Elements (Actuators):** Implement changes, such as control valves, heaters, or variable speed pumps.
- **Human Machine Interface (HMI):** Allows operators to monitor and interact with the process.
**Types of Process Control**

- **Continuous:** Smooth, uninterrupted control (e.g., oil refining).
- **Batch:** Distinct quantities combined for specific durations (e.g., food/beverage production).
- **Discrete:** Manufacturing individual pieces (e.g., metal stamping).

**Benefits and Applications**  
Process control systems are essential for improving efficiency, reducing variability in product quality, and ensuring safety in industries like petrochemical, pharmaceutical, and food processing. They allow for centralized control of complex operations.
