A Transaction Processing System (TPS) in business is ==an information system that captures, processes, stores, and retrieves data from routine, day-to-day operational transactions==. It ensures accuracy and speed in handling activities like sales, inventory updates, payroll, or bank transfers, providing a foundation for higher-level management systems.

**Key Aspects of a TPS in Business:**

- **Purpose:** To automate and manage high-volume, routine transactions efficiently.
- **Examples:** Point-of-sale ([[Study/1st Semester/CSIT/POS\|POS]]) systems, ATM transactions, airline booking systems, and payroll processing.
- **Types of Processing:**
    - **Real-time Processing:** Data is processed immediately (e.g., online shopping).
    - **Batch Processing:** Transactions are collected and processed together at a scheduled time (e.g., end-of-day payroll).
- **Key Characteristics:**
    - **Performance:** Requires fast, rapid response times.
    - **Reliability:** Systems must be highly dependable, as failure can halt operations.
    - **Consistency:** Standardized, inflexible procedures to ensure data integrity.
**Benefits:**  
TPS increases operational efficiency, improves accuracy, enables rapid decision-making based on real-time data, and supports customer service
