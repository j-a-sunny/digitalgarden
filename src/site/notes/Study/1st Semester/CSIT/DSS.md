A **Decision Support System ([[Study/1st Semester/CSIT/DSS\|DSS]])** in business is a computerized program that analyze large sets of data to help leaders make informed, data-driven decisions. By combining data, models, and analytical tools, a [[Study/1st Semester/CSIT/DSS\|DSS]] supports decision-making in non-routine, complex, or semi-structured situations, such as sales forecasting, inventory management, or strategic planning.

## Key Aspects of a Business DSS:

- **Functionality:** It gathers data from multiple sources to create interactive, user-friendly reports and visualizations, moving beyond simple data collection.
- **Capabilities:** It uses predictive modeling, "what-if" analysis (simulating outcomes of different decisions), and data mining to identify patterns.
- **Types of Decisions:** Primarily supports tactical and strategic, semi-structured, or unstructured decisions, rather than routine operational decisions.
- **Types of DSS:**
    - **Data-Driven:** Focuses on access and manipulation of internal company data and external data.
    - **Model-Driven:** Uses analytical, simulation, or optimization models to analyze scenarios.
    - **Knowledge-Driven:** Uses artificial intelligence and machine learning for recommendations.

- **Improved Decision Speed & Quality:** Enables faster analysis of complex, high-volume data.
- **Increased Efficiency:** Streamlines decision-making processes and reduces reliance on gut feeling.
- **Competitive Advantage:** Helps identify new market opportunities or operational improvements.
