A **Knowledge Management System (KMS)** in business is a technology-based platform or set of processes designed to capture, store, organize, and share an organization's collective information, expertise, and documents. It acts as a central repository, enhancing productivity by making it easy for employees to retrieve critical knowledge.

**Key Aspects of a KMS in Business:**

- **Purpose:** The primary goal is to improve decision-making, boost operational efficiency, and prevent the loss of institutional knowledge.
- **Core Components:** It typically includes knowledge repositories (documents, databases), search/retrieval tools, and collaboration tools.
- **Benefits:**
    - **Reduced Training Time:** Facilitates faster employee onboarding.
    - **Improved Service:** Empowers customer service teams to resolve issues faster.
    - **Enhanced Collaboration:** Promotes sharing of expertise across teams.
- **Applications:** Commonly used in customer service, HR, legal, and compliance-heavy industries to create a "single source of truth".
- **Modern Features:** Many KMS solutions now use AI for automated tagging and content recommendations. 

Popular examples include [Monday.com](https://monday.com/blog/service/knowledge-management-system/), [Zendesk](https://www.zendesk.com/in/service/help-center/knowledge-management-system/), [Salesforce](https://www.salesforce.com/ap/service/what-is-knowledge-management/system/), and [HubSpot](https://www.hubspot.com/knowledge-management-systems)
